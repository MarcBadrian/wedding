On October 10th, 2015, I asked Michelle to marry me.

We had planned a trip to Disney World for the Food and Wine Festival, and although we had talked about waiting until after I graduated with my Master's degree before getting engaged, there was no way I could pass up the opportunity. Michelle's a Disney kid through and through, so I knew that proposing at Disney World could really be something magical.

I had also said I wouldn't propose at Disney unless I could make it my own and make it unique, so I set out to do just that. It took me a while and some advice from friends, but I found a 3rd party company outside of Disney to set up a special scavenger hunt throughout the Magic Kingdom for Michelle and me. The twist was that I made it look like Michelle and I had been "randomly selected" to participate in a 'Disney Dream Days Challenge Sweepstakes' - a race to find clues in Magic Kingdom for a chance to win some big prizes.

To make it look legitimate, I designed and printed an official sweepstakes invitation, included over six pages of official rules and regulations, and added release forms that we both had to sign and turn in to the coordinators. Then I worked with the staff at Disney's Port Orleans hotel where we were staying to have it all waiting for us in our room when we arrived.


Michelle wasn't really into it when we first found the invitation. She thought it might be lame or we that wouldn't win, and only really agreed to do it for me, because she knows I like that kind of stuff. So I said "Okay sure yeah, do it for me." Thankfully she came around, and quickly got excited for the scavenger hunt as we set off on our adventure.

As it turned out, the scavenger hunt was actually pretty hard (I even had to get some hints texted to me to make sure that we made it to the end in time). When we finally reached the last task of the sweepstakes, we were told to draw our best Mickey Mouse while blindfolded. Waiting in the wings, Michelle's sister Pamela and her cousin Rachel showed up to surprise her, cameras ready to catch the big moment on tape. Once Michelle started drawing, I took off my blindfold and got down on one knee. I asked, she said yes, and here we are!

The Bride's Reaction

Honestly, I didn't see it coming at all! Marc and I had spoken several times about how we had a timeline for when it came to taking the next step in our relationship. He wanted to wait until after he got his Master's Degree and saved up a little bit of money. So I didn't think that a proposal was coming my way for at least another year! When I took that blindfold off and saw him down on one knee, I was in TOTAL DISBELIEF. It's the only time that anyone has ever completely surprised me. I swear to you that I had an out of body experience and didn't believe that any of it was real or happening. I felt so bad watching the video back and seeing my reaction. I can't believe I left the guy hanging! He will never let that one go, and I will never leave him hanging again!
